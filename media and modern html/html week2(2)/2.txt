
Certainly! Here are five commonly used HTML entities:

&lt; - Less than (<)

Represents the less than symbol (<), commonly used in HTML tags.
&gt; - Greater than (>)

Represents the greater than symbol (>), commonly used in HTML tags.
&amp; - Ampersand (&)

Represents the ampersand symbol (&), used to start character entities and escape the actual ampersand in text.
&quot; - Double quotation mark (")

Represents the double quotation mark (") and is often used within HTML attributes.
&apos; - Single quotation mark or apostrophe (')

Represents the single quotation mark or apostrophe (') and is less commonly used compared to &quot;.
